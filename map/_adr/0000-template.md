## Context

The forces at play. What problem are you solving, what constraints bind you,
what was true before, and what made a decision necessary now? Give enough that a
reader who wasn't there feels the pressure you were under. This is usually the
longest section.

## Decision

What you decided, stated plainly and in the present tense ("X is Y"). Include
the explicit *no*s — what you chose **not** to do is often the most useful
record.

## Consequences

What follows from the decision — good and bad. What becomes easy, what becomes
hard, what you are now committed to, what you have foreclosed. Be honest about
the costs; an ADR listing only upsides is not trustworthy.

## Alternatives considered

The options you rejected and why — but only when the rejection is non-obvious.
If you weighed two reasonable approaches and picked one for subtle reasons,
record them here so nobody re-proposes the loser later.
